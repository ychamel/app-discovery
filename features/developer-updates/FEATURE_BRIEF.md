## Coordinator scope seed (source: signal-capture SC-7/SC-8, OQ-4 — net-new, not in the breakdown)

> Facts carried over for traceability. The Product Analyst owns the brief below; this block
> is context, not the brief.

- **Layer / build phase:** Developer-facing · Phase 3 (Dev value)
- **Purpose:** Give a developer a channel to **communicate with their subscribed users** —
  post updates, ask about needs / problems, offer **early-access** — so the platform becomes
  the developer's **front page** even when they already have their own website. The
  developer-side **reason to use the platform**, which (paired with subscriptions) generates
  the on-platform engagement `signal-capture` records.
- **MVP slice:** A developer posts an update / early-access note to an app's subscribers;
  subscribers are notified and pulled back to the platform to engage. Builds the *surface*;
  `signal-capture` records the resulting return / re-engagement events.
- **Proves (hypothesis):** H2 (developers reach and engage their audience on-platform)
- **Depends on:** app-subscriptions (needs subscribers to post to), app-pages, signal-capture
- **Vision design ref:** §6 Dev-facing, §8 ("devs spend less time on marketing")
- **Provenance:** Spawned at signal-capture's Stage-1 review (2026-06-18). The SC-7 pivot
  made the corpus depend on on-platform engagement; SC-8 held the engagement *surfaces* out
  of `signal-capture` (OQ-4). This is the developer-side half. **Pairs with** and **depends
  on** `app-subscriptions` (the user-side half — there must be subscribers before a developer
  can address them).
- **Source:** [features/signal-capture/OPEN_QUESTIONS.md](../signal-capture/OPEN_QUESTIONS.md) OQ-4;
  [features/signal-capture/DECISIONS.md](../signal-capture/DECISIONS.md) SC-7/SC-8.
