### `Q-003`: Should a user be able to follow their own app?

- **Reporter:** User
- **Date Reported:** 2026-06-29
- **Category:** Product
- **Status:** `IN-PROGRESS`
- **Patch Slug:** [`patch-block-self-interaction`](../patches/patch-block-self-interaction/) (bundled with `Q-002`)

#### The Question / Ambiguity
The developer who submitted an app can currently click "Follow" on their own app's detail page. The follow mechanic presumably sends the follower notifications about new updates, curator notes, or activity on the app. Is it meaningful — or redundant / confusing — for the developer to follow their own app?

#### Context & Alternatives
The developer is *the source* of app updates. They author them; they do not need to receive notifications about their own posts. Following one's own app:
- Wastes a notification channel (dev gets pinged about changes they made).
- Adds noise to the follower count metric (the developer's own follow inflates it by 1, which can mislead curators or other users reading the count).

**Option A — Block self-follow (recommended):** Hide or disable the "Follow" button when `request.user == app.developer`. No follow record is created; follower count reflects only external interest.

**Option B — Allow but suppress notifications:** Let the follow record exist but exclude the developer from the notification fanout for their own app's events.

**Option C — Keep as-is:** No restriction. Minimal implementation risk; accept the inflated count as negligible noise.

#### Proposed Resolution / Answer
**Answered 2026-06-29 → Option A (block self-follow)** via DN-Q003. Hide/disable the Follow
button when `request.user == app.owner` *and* reject the follow mutation server-side (no
follow record is created; the follower count reflects only external interest). Routed to the
Patch Track as [`patch-block-self-interaction`](../patches/patch-block-self-interaction/),
bundled with `Q-002` (symmetrical owner-guard). No schema change — owner identity is already
on the `CatalogApp` read-model.
