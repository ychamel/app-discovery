### `Q-004`: Should apps display an aggregate review score, and should curated ratings be shown separately?

- **Reporter:** User
- **Date Reported:** 2026-06-29
- **Category:** Product
- **Status:** `NEW`

#### The Question / Ambiguity
After one or more reviews are submitted for an app, should the app detail page (and catalog card) show an **average/aggregate score**? And if so, should the platform distinguish between **curator ratings** (from vetted curators) and **community ratings** (from regular users)?

#### Context & Alternatives
The platform has a dual trust model: curators are vetted experts whose endorsement is the core value proposition; regular user reviews add social proof but carry less weight. Mixing them into a single average could dilute the curator signal.

**On aggregate display:**

**Option A — Show a single average across all reviews:** Simple to implement and familiar (star rating). Risk: a flood of low-effort community ratings drowns curator signal.

**Option B — Show two distinct scores:** e.g., "Curator Score: 4.8 ★ (3 curators)" + "Community Score: 3.9 ★ (42 users)". Preserves the platform's differentiation but requires two separate aggregate computations and two UI slots.

**Option C — Curated score only on catalog, community score on detail page:** Catalog cards show only the curated average (the trust signal that drives discovery); the full detail page shows both. Balances noise vs. signal at each touch point.

**Option D — No aggregate score at all (editorial model):** Some curation platforms (e.g., Pitchfork) skip numeric aggregates and rely on written editorial curation. Fits the platform's high-quality positioning but removes a fast-scanning affordance.

#### Proposed Resolution / Answer (To be filled during triage)
[Awaiting product decision. Recommendation: Option B or C — both preserve the curator/community distinction that is central to the platform's value prop. Option C adds minimal complexity while keeping catalog cards clean. Cross-reference the ranking algorithm ADR in DECISIONS.md before implementing, as the aggregate score may feed into ranking.]
