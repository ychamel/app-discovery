### `UX-004`: "My Submissions" label is misleading after app approval; no status-based grouping

- **Reporter:** User
- **Date Reported:** 2026-06-29
- **Priority:** Medium
- **Status:** `NEW`
- **Patch/Feature Slug:** `TBD`

#### UX Observation
The developer-facing section that lists submitted apps is labelled **"My Submissions"** even after an app has been approved and is live on the platform. "Submission" implies a pending or in-review state; calling an approved, active listing a "submission" is semantically incorrect and creates cognitive dissonance for the developer.

Additionally, all apps are shown in a flat list with no visual separation by lifecycle state. A developer cannot quickly tell which apps are live, which are awaiting review, and which have been rejected.

#### Impact & User Friction
- Developers lose confidence that their app actually went live because the label never changes.
- Scanning a list of apps across different states (active, under review, rejected) is slow and error-prone without grouping or status tags.
- The current label undercuts the reward loop: a developer who gets an app approved should feel that milestone clearly.

#### Suggested Improvement
1. **Rename the section** from "My Submissions" → **"My Apps"** (or "My Listed Apps").
2. **Add a visible status badge/tag** to each app card (`Active`, `Under Review`, `Rejected`, `Draft`).
3. **Group or filter by status**: either tabs (`All | Active | Under Review | Rejected`) or collapsible section headers per status category.
4. The rejected group should show a brief reason or a link to the rejection note if available.

#### Visual References / Markup
- Developer dashboard section: likely rendered by the template handling `/developer/apps` or similar route.
- Status values to map: `approved`/`active`, `pending`/`under_review`, `rejected`.
